## The registered prediction failed on its primary clause — and the
## registered failure branch is the finding

**CS co-attack stayed at 1.00 in every run, D included.** No agent wrote
a stratified countersubject in the exposition, with the Contrapunctus IX
rule stated as rule 3b, a worked harmonized/stratified pair in the
reference, and the recorded strata-004 lesson all in context. Meanwhile
the piece-level homorhythm means did drop (D: 0.52, 0.42 — f-D-3 is the
lowest in the entire corpus, below every warm capstone) via syncopated
chains and complementary clocks in the episodes and middle entries.

The design.md failure branch, verbatim: "If D instead reproduces the
pack's homorhythmic exposition, the exemplar's gravity beats the named
rule, and the next iteration must re-record the worked exposition itself
with a stratified CS." That is what happened, and it sharpens the 005
conclusion into the series' cleanest law:

**Cold agents compose what the examples SHOW, not what the rules SAY —
and they apply example material at the structural position where the
example showed it.** The weave lesson showed suspensions in a drill
against a cantus; agents deploy suspensions in free episodes (where a
drill-shaped figure fits). The exposition exemplar shows a homorhythmic
CS at bars 8-15; agents reproduce it at bars 8-15 regardless of any rule
adjacent to it. The strata lesson showed a drill, not an exposition — so
strata appear in the texture generally (homorhythm means fell) but never
at the one location owned by a stronger exemplar.
